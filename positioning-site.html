The Problem

You have ideas everywhere.
Your AI has no idea where to find them.

Notes in one app. Documents in another. A folder of files you forgot about. When you ask AI for help, you end up copying and pasting random context and hoping for the best.

Whiteboards are great for brainstorming but produce nothing structured. Document editors are great for writing but terrible for seeing how ideas connect.

flo bridges both. Think visually on a canvas. Add detail only where it's needed. Export everything as a single structured file your AI actually understands. No folder management. No file juggling. Just your ideas, organized.

Four card types. Each one means something.

Cards aren't generic sticky notes. Each type tells flo — and your AI — what kind of information it holds.

Context Scoping

You decide what your AI sees.

Every reference link has a scope. Instead of dumping everything into a prompt, you control the granularity. flo handles the rest.

Title only
Just the card name. A quick "see also" mention. Minimal context.
Summary
Card title + the short text on its face. Enough for orientation.
Specific section
A targeted heading from the card's document. Surgical precision.
Full card
Everything — the complete card and its full document. Use sparingly.

Who It's For

For anyone who thinks in systems
but doesn't think in file systems.

You don't need to be technical to use flo. If you can arrange sticky notes on a wall, you can use this. The structured output happens automatically.

Stop copy-pasting random context into ChatGPT

You know AI works better with good context, but organizing that context is a nightmare. flo does it for you. Drop your ideas on the canvas, connect them, and export — your AI gets a structured briefing instead of a wall of text.

  • No file management needed
  • Visual organization, not folder organization
  • One-click export for any AI tool
  • Quality checks catch problems automatically
Founders & Strategists

Turn complex plans into something an AI can actually help with

You're juggling product strategy, hiring plans, fundraising decks, and competitive research. Map the whole picture spatially, then hand your AI the context it needs to give you useful answers — not generic ones.

  • See the big picture and the details
  • Typed cards match how you think
  • Canvas view + list view for different modes
  • Documents stay connected to decisions

  • One map per client, everything in one place
  • Brainstorm cards hidden from AI exports
  • Reference scoping prevents info overload
  • Local and private — client data stays on your machine
